information architecture

Information architecture is the foundational stage of producing a user-centered website. It involves organizing, structuring, and labeling content to enhance user understanding and navigation. By researching previous personal websites, I identify the needs of visitors and create a clear roadmap to ensure a logical flow of information. The information in the site must be personally relevant to me, including personal experiences, personal design work, and accomplishments. This process helps to achieve an intuitive user experience that allows visitors to effortlessly find information and promotes engagement.

Navigation design is an important aspect of the user experience. We carefully plan and implement a navigation structure that guides users seamlessly through the site. I created seven navigation links corresponding to the content pages. At the same time, through the application of JavaScript dynamic effects and Bootstrap5, I created navigation sidebars that were able to incorporate a visually appealing design while still being functional. Simple, clear menus, intuitive links, and thoughtful placement enhance user accessibility. The navigation allows users to easily navigate between pages, sections, and content, ensuring positive and engaging interactions.

Wireframe

Wireframing is the blueprint stage of realizing a concept. Through visual sketches, I map out the skeletal structure of each page, outlining key elements and their placement. I segment the content of each page through different sections, adding visual variety to the site while ensuring reading order and flow. As for the wireframe of the overall webpage, I referred to news websites such as BBC, LOL Esports, etc., and imitated their methods and ideas of dividing the information content.

Visual elements (including graphics, images and multimedia) play a key role in creating memorable and immersive user experiences. For the homepage, I chose a combination of large images and looping videos to showcase highlights of the site's content, a design that quickly grabs the user's attention and encourages them to continue their exploration through visual and auditory feedback. For the interior pages, I focused more on the use of text and images to enrich the browsing experience and informational content, ensuring that effective messages were delivered. I pay attention to detail in image selection, color palette and multimedia integration. White and black, as the main colors of my website, are not only simple to use, but the simplified color scheme also effectively highlights the key content of each page and avoids the visual obstacles caused by complex colors.

Accessibility and AODA compliance

Regarding WCAG 2.0 (Web Content Accessibility Guidelines Guidelines), this website provides text alternative content for non-text content, incorporating adaptable content with distinguishable content. The site was created based on keyboard accessibility and is committed to providing users with sufficient time to read and use the content. The site is not designed in a way that is known to cause seizures. The site incorporates navigable content (e.g., home page, featured jobs, resume, etc.) that provides readable content in a straightforward typographic format. The site has predictable pages and the sites are compatible with each other.

We provide text-alternative content for non-text elements to ensure screen reader accessibility. Our content is designed to be adaptable, allowing users to customize the experience to their needs. We ensure that content is presented in a distinguishable and easily perceived manner. Our website is designed to be navigated and used using only a keyboard, providing support for users who rely on keyboard navigation. Providing users with ample time to read and use the content ensures a more inclusive experience. The design of our website avoids elements known to cause seizures. Our page design is predictable and consistent in terms of layout and navigation. Our website is compatible with a variety of assistive technologies to ensure broad accessibility.
